How to Play Truco

Truco is a traditional Argentine card game played with a 40-card Spanish deck. Each player is dealt 3 cards per hand. The goal is to win 2 out of 3 rounds using higher-ranked cards or special calls like Truco, Envido, and Flor.

Card Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest)

  1. 1 of Swords (Ancho de Espadas)
  2. 1 of Clubs (Ancho de Bastos)
  3. 7 of Swords
  4. 7 of Gold
  5. All 3s
  6. All 2s
  7. 1 of Cups & 1 of Coins
  8. All 12s (Kings)
  9. All 11s (Knights)
  10. All 10s (Jacks)
  11. 7 of Cups & 7 of Clubs
  12. All 6s
  13. All 5s
  14. All 4s

Gameplay Flow

  • Each player receives 3 cards.
  • Highest card wins each round.
  • Winning 2 of 3 rounds wins the hand.
  • Points accumulate to 15 or 30 depending on variation.

Special Calls

  • Truco: Raises hand value to 2 points. Can escalate to Retruco and Vale Cuatro.
  • Envido: Side bet using two cards of the same suit + 20. Max score is 33.
  • Flor: All 3 cards of same suit — automatically beats Envido.

Tips

  • Use bluffing even with weak cards.
  • Observe facial expressions and hesitation.
  • Play Envido early when strong in a suit.
  • Fold when necessary — avoid giving extra points.
